## 1. Introduction

This Data Processing Addendum ("DPA") supplements and forms part of the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy between LedgerAI ("Processor," "we," "us," or "our") and you ("Controller," "Customer," "you," or "your") when you use our Service.

This DPA applies when:
- You are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or another jurisdiction with similar data protection laws
- You require a DPA to comply with GDPR, UK GDPR, or other applicable data protection regulations
- You process personal data through our Service

## 2. Definitions

For purposes of this DPA:

- **"Controller"** means the entity that determines the purposes and means of processing personal data
- **"Processor"** means the entity that processes personal data on behalf of the Controller
- **"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person
- **"Processing"** means any operation performed on personal data (collection, storage, use, disclosure, etc.)
- **"GDPR"** means the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679
- **"UK GDPR"** means the UK General Data Protection Regulation
- **"Data Subject"** means the natural person to whom personal data relates
- **"Sub-processor"** means a third party engaged by the Processor to process personal data

## 3. Roles and Responsibilities

### 3.1 Controller Responsibilities

As the Controller, you are responsible for:
- Determining the purposes and means of processing personal data
- Ensuring you have a lawful basis for processing personal data
- Obtaining necessary consents from data subjects
- Complying with applicable data protection laws
- Providing accurate instructions for processing
- Ensuring the personal data you provide is accurate and up-to-date

### 3.2 Processor Responsibilities

As the Processor, we will:
- Process personal data only on your documented instructions
- Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal data
- Assist you in complying with your obligations under applicable data protection laws
- Notify you of any data breaches without undue delay
- Assist with data subject rights requests
- Provide information necessary to demonstrate compliance

## 5. Data Processing Instructions

### 5.1 Processing Only on Instructions

We will process personal data only:

- As necessary to provide the Service
- As required by applicable law (in which case we will inform you, unless prohibited by law)

### 5.2 Prohibited Processing

We will not:
- Process personal data for purposes other than those specified in this DPA
- Sell personal data to third parties
- Use personal data for our own purposes beyond providing the Service
- Retain personal data longer than necessary

## 6. Technical and Organizational Measures

### 6.1 Security Measures

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including:

**Technical Measures:**
- Encryption of data in transit (TLS/SSL)
- Encryption of data at rest
- Access controls and authentication
- Regular security assessments and penetration testing
- Intrusion detection and monitoring
- Backup and disaster recovery procedures

**Organizational Measures:**
- Limited access on a need-to-know basis
- Employee training on data protection
- Confidentiality agreements with personnel
- Regular security audits
- Incident response procedures

### 6.2 Data Center Security

Personal data is stored in secure data centers operated by:
- **Google Cloud Platform (Firebase):** SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001 certified
- **Stripe:** PCI DSS Level 1 compliant

## 10. Data Breach Notification

### 10.1 Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach, we will:
- Notify you without undue delay (and in any event within 72 hours of becoming aware)
- Provide you with information necessary to comply with your notification obligations
- Assist you in investigating and remediating the breach

### 10.2 Breach Information

Our notification will include:
- Description of the nature of the breach
- Categories and approximate number of data subjects affected
- Categories and approximate number of personal data records concerned
- Likely consequences of the breach
- Measures taken or proposed to address the breach
